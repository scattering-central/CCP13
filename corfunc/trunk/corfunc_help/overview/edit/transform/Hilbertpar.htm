3.1.5 Hilbert Transform Parameters

Figure 1: Hilbert transform parameters

These parameters are used in the Transform process, to generate a normalised segment density distribution (a volume fraction profile) from the extrapolated data. The user must specify the dimensions of the q-axis (as reciprocal Angstroms or reciprocal nanometres), the bulk density of the adsorbed surfactant or polymer (in grams per cubic centimetre, >0.5 & <2.0), and the amount of surfactant or polymer adsorbed (in milligrams per square metre, >0.0). If values corresponding to different dimensions are used the normalisation will not be correct.

The Normalisation accuracy (>0.0 & <1.0) is the maximum permitted deviation between the adsorbed amount entered and the re-scaled integral under the volume fraction profile (see Normalisation). Thus in Figure 1, normalisation would stop once the integral was between 0.99 - 1.01.
